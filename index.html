Brand
Story

The best
seat is
yours.

FrontRow Africa exists because Africa's live entertainment deserves more than an afterthought — it deserves the front row.

Built by Creative Spills, FrontRow Africa is premium Afrocentric ticketing infrastructure for concerts, comedy nights, cultural showcases, and intimate live events across the continent. From Nairobi to Lagos, from Accra to Johannesburg — this platform was built to reflect the full breadth and ambition of African live culture.

The design language is the same deliberate signal: warm earth tones drawn from African textiles, kente-inspired geometry, bold type that commands a stage. Every decision says the same thing — this was made by us, for us, about us.

FrontRow Africa isn't just a ticket. It's the first act of a night you'll never forget.

Wordmark Anatomy

  • Front is always rendered in bone white or black — it anchors the brand name and commands attention.
  • Row shifts to the accent colour (Kente Gold or Adire Indigo) to create rhythm and hierarchy within the stacked mark.
  • Africa in small-caps mono sits below the main mark — always present in the primary lockup. It may be dropped at very small sizes (icon use only).
  • The Dot Mark is a deliberate terracotta circle — never an asterisk, never decorative. It is the reserved seat. It is yours.
  • Never stretch, rotate, or alter the weight of any element in the wordmark.
  • Minimum print size: 24px. Minimum digital display height: 20px.

08

Voice
& Tone

FrontRow Africa speaks like the most plugged-in person in the room — knowledgeable, warm, and never pretentious. Premium without being cold. Continental without being vague.

✓   Do Write This
"Sauti Sol are back. One night. Kasarani. This is the show you'll tell people you were at."
Confident. Present tense. Understands what this moment means culturally.
"Front Row Presale closes at midnight — after that, it's General. At double the price."
Honest urgency. No hype. Just facts that make the decision easy.
"Tickets confirmed. Gates open 18:30. See you at the front. 🎤"
Warm, peer-level. Closes the loop with energy, not a disclaimer.
✗   Don't Write This
"We are pleased to present an unmissable evening of world-class entertainment."
Generic. Zero specificity. Could describe anything. Not FrontRow.
"HURRY!!! TICKETS SELLING FAST!!! DON'T MISS OUT!!!"
Desperate energy. Excessive punctuation signals low trust.
"Your booking reference number has been successfully processed."
Bank language. Kills the excitement of buying a ticket. Never do this.
Voice Principles
01
Specific
Name the artist, the city, the venue. Specificity builds trust. "Kasarani Stadium, Nairobi" beats "a premier venue" every time.
02
Present
Events are now. The energy is now. Write in the present tense wherever possible. The front row doesn't wait.
03
Earned
Premium never shouts. Our confidence comes from knowing the culture deeply — not from asserting that we're the best.
04
Continental
We speak to all of Africa. From Lagos to Nairobi to Accra — our voice recognises the full breadth of the continent's live scene.

Imagery
Guidelines

Photography must feel editorial, alive, and specifically African. FrontRow Africa's premium positioning demands imagery that matches — real moments, real faces, real African stages.

🎤
Artist on stage
Intimate venue lighting
🥁
Audience energy
Candid crowd moments
🎶
Venue texture
Warm ambient light
✨
Artist close-ups
Natural, editorial feel
🎸
Backstage moments
Authentic, unposed
🌍
City & venue exterior
Nairobi context
✓   Use imagery that
  • Captures real moments from real African events — candid over staged
  • Includes warm, golden tones that echo the FrontRow Africa palette
  • Shows African faces, community, and collective energy prominently
  • Uses rich venue atmosphere: stage glow, crowd depth, ambient haze
  • Reflects the continent broadly — not one city, not one scene
  • Crops tightly and asymmetrically — editorial, not event catalogue
✗   Avoid imagery that
  • Looks like stock photography — clean, staged, generic
  • Uses bright white or harsh studio lighting
  • Shows only European faces without diversity
  • Has heavy, garish filters or Instagram-style overlays
  • Centers the venue over the people and the energy
  • Is low resolution or pixelated — quality matters
